About Suhas Roy

A Journey Through Art and Expression (1936-2016)

1936 - 2016

The Artist's Journey

Born in 1936 in Bengal, Suhas Roy emerged as one of India's most distinguished artists. His artistic journey began at the Indian College of Arts and Draftsmanship, Kolkata, where he graduated in painting in 1958. His talent and dedication to art led him to Paris in 1965, where he studied graphic art and murals under a French government exchange programme.

Roy's artistic career was marked by significant roles in prestigious institutions. He became a member of the Society of Contemporary Artists, Calcutta in 1960, and later served as the principal of Indian College of Art and Draftsmanship from 1971 to 1973. His academic journey culminated at Kala Bhavan, Santiniketan, where he held the position of Head of the Department of Painting.

Artistic Style & Vision

Roy's artistic vision was deeply rooted in the exploration of the female form and face. His works are characterized by a unique blend of sensuality and honesty, creating dreamlike compositions that bridge reality and fantasy. The mystical quality in his art emerged from his profound interpretations of the 'unknown', while drawing inspiration from the life around him.

Artistic Legacy

Suhas Roy's legacy lives on through his remarkable body of work that continues to inspire artists and art enthusiasts alike. His unique interpretation of the female form, combined with his masterful technique and mystical approach, has left an indelible mark on Indian contemporary art. Roy lived and worked in Kolkata until his passing in 2016, leaving behind a rich artistic heritage that continues to influence the art world.
